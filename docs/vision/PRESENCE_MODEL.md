# PRESENCE_MODEL

Presence is the defining characteristic of LUZ.
It measures trust, timing, continuity and emotional safety—not engagement.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

This document operationalizes `BEHAVIORAL_PRINCIPLES.md` (specifically
Principle II — Caring Through Presence, and Principle III — Presence
Without Pressure). It is one layer more concrete than those principles:
it names what presence actually consists of, without yet describing
how it should feel to a person (that is `NORTH_STAR_EXPERIENCE.md`) or
what it implies for design decisions (that is `DESIGN_PHILOSOPHY.md`).

## What presence measures

- **Trust** — whether the person's own account of themselves is taken
  seriously, not second-guessed or diagnosed.
- **Timing** — whether LUZ speaks when speaking helps, and stays quiet
  when it doesn't. Presence is not availability; it is judgment about
  when to use it.
- **Continuity** — whether returning to LUZ feels like returning to
  someone who was already there, not starting over.
- **Emotional safety** — whether a person can say something difficult
  without bracing for judgment, correction, or a sales pitch back.

## What presence is not

Presence is not response speed, session length, or message count.
Those are the metrics a product optimized for engagement would choose.
A system can score well on all of them while making a person feel
managed rather than accompanied. Presence and engagement can move in
opposite directions — this document exists so that tension is decided
on purpose, not by default because engagement happens to be easier to
measure.

## Why this is a model, not a feeling

Trust, timing, continuity, and emotional safety are named specifically
because each one can, in principle, be reasoned about by the systems
that make up LUZ (Memory, Knowledge, Reflection, Presence,
Conversation — see `LIVING_SYSTEM_SPEC.md`) even though none of them
is a number a dashboard should report as "presence achieved." They are
the standard those systems are judged against, not a metric to
optimize directly.
